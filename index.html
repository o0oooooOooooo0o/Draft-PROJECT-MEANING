Heart Coherence
Quick Coherence® Technique · HeartMath Institute
The heart generates the body's largest electromagnetic field — measurably detectable several feet away. Heart Rate Variability (HRV) reflects autonomic nervous system regulation: in coherence, HRV becomes smooth and rhythmically ordered. This entrains brain waves, cortisol, and cognition.
01
Heart-Focused Breathing
Shift your attention to the area of the heart. Imagine your breath flowing in and out through your heart or chest area — breathing a little slower and deeper than usual.
→ Inhale 5 sec · Exhale 5 sec · 6 breaths/min · resonance at ~0.1 Hz
02
Activate a Regenerative Feeling
Make a sincere attempt to experience appreciation, care, or ease. Re-experience a real feeling: a person you love, a pet, a place, an accomplishment. A small genuine feeling outperforms a forced grand one.
→ Sincerity is the active ingredient. Both steps can merge into one breath cycle.

Reflective State Transfer
Stage Two · Mirror Interaction Protocol
The mirror is a state-bearing self-model: a version of you that already embodies the condition you want to induce. Because Stage 1 has loaded sensory realism, this interaction is no longer vague visualization. The target state transfers through felt contact, using the same perceptual channels the brain already treats as real input.
What does the mirror carry?
One word or phrase. This is the condition the mirror embodies more strongly than you do right now. Leave blank for "the version of me I need."
Important
This is a state-change and symptom-support technique — not a substitute for medical care. It can help with pain modulation, calm, confidence, stress, and functional access to states. It cannot diagnose or treat disease. If you have a serious condition, continue working with your clinicians. Stop if the practice becomes distressing.

Sensory Reality Loading
Imaginary Sensory Induction Protocol · Pure Perceptual Training
The brain treats perceived reality as actionable context. This protocol trains one thing only: imagined sensory input → increased reality-weight → stronger perceptual uptake. One sense is isolated per window, loaded as vividly as possible, and held until the brain begins responding as if it were more real. No symbolism. No emotion target. No identity target. Only disciplined sensory activation, one channel at a time.
Opening Instruction
"For the next few minutes, I am training my brain to experience imagined sensation as real enough to register."
No emotional target. No identity target. No story. Only prepare to sense.
